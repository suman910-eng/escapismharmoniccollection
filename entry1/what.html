Is everything just...escapism?
Escapism is defined as 'the tendency to seek distraction and relief from unpleasant realities, especially by seeking entertainment or engaging in fantasy.'

Having in mind that probably everyone will sooner or later die, isn't living just a way to 'seek relief' from the 'unpleasant reality' of death?

On the other hand, for those who accept and acknowledge death as unavoidable, isn't this acceptance also just another form of escapism?

People are probably aquainted with the emptiness we humans feel inside. From what I gather on this matter, the consensus is that this emptiness cannot be filled in this world, and that by avoiding the emptiness and by focusing on other aspects of life, one can reduce the size of the hole considerably. However, don't we avoid this emptiness only because it would be too harsh to live in it? Isn't this another form of escaping a reality?

What even is a 'reality'? It is defined as 'the state of things as they actually exist (...)'. But there is so much we don't know about life, about death, about us, about others, about how all these 'actually exist'. There's a quote from Neon Genesis Evangelion saying that 'The truthes are as many as people. But there's only one truth that you have, which is formed of the narrowed view of the world, that's revised information to protect yourself, the twisted truth.' In other words, by the single act of living, each of us create our own reality in order to protect and distract ourselves: Escapism. What are we trying to escape from, anyway?
